Evolving value models

Vision

Innovative business models, agility to meet demand, green financing, and balanced trade will drive manufacturing

Scope

Understanding, design, customisation, and adoption of increasingly complex or even disruptive organisational business and value models

UK strengths

The UK is well set to take advantage with its net zero policy and strategy, commitment to growth in research and development, funding for new business models, and strength in high-tech manufacturing

Global outlook

Intro

In 2050, society demands that businesses optimise environmental, economic, and societal impacts of their products and operations. Customers specify what they want, leading to universal demand for individual and customised products. Users are integral to conception and design. Manufacturing has responded to meet these complex requirements with flexible and adaptable systems. This requires the understanding, design, customisation, and adoption of increasingly complex business models.

Coordinated and pertinent research

The UK’s respected research base and national commitments and a vibrant private investment market can grow research and development and provide the environment to ensure organisations can transform with managed and understood risk.

Trends and challenges

Promissing trends are impact management. Other trends are industrial R&D returns, academic research output.

Targeted and accessible financing

The UK can build on existing export strengths to deliver these business models to new markets and secure long-term growth.

Trends and challenges

Promissing trends are VC platform services, social innovation. Other trends are British Business Bank facilitation, being force for good, corporate social responsibility, environmental, social and governance (ESG).

Innovative business models

The increased servitisation of products plays to UK strengths. UK companies lead the way in this business model. The UK also has a very strong service sector providing transferable skills and capabilities that can unlock value.

Trends and challenges

Promissing trends are hyper-customisation, manufacturing as a service, MaaS, shifting product ownership, cloud services. Other trends are complex value tools, end-to-end models, value framework, business model innovation, technology stacks, user-centricity, servitisation, XaaS, tracking usage data, commercialisation. There are also challenges such as tackling wicked problems, energy-food-water stress nexus, business model ownerships, user-data privacy.

Open and vibrant trade

A stable policy framework and long-term targets, such as net zero, give confidence to UK companies to invest in next-generation business models and to meet the opportunities presented by the changing demands of customers.

Trends and challenges

Other trends are foreign direct investment. There are also challenges such as global systemic competition, fair trading.
